# Observability Fundamentals

Observability is the ability to understand the internal state of a system by looking at its outputs.

## The three pillars of observability
1. Logs
   - Record events and errors.
   - Useful for debugging and investigating issues.

2. Metrics
   - Numeric values collected over time.
   - Useful for monitoring trends, health, and performance.

3. Traces
   - Show the path of a request across services.
   - Useful for finding latency bottlenecks and failures.

## Why observability matters
It helps teams to:
- detect issues quickly
- identify root causes
- understand performance problems
- improve reliability and user experience

## Quick summary
A system is observable when you can answer:
- What is happening?
- Why is it happening?
- Is it healthy or failing?

## Interview-friendly note
Observability is not just monitoring. It is the broader practice of understanding systems through logs, metrics, and traces.

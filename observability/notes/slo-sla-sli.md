# SLI, SLO, and SLA

These terms are commonly used in service reliability and observability.

## SLI - Service Level Indicator
An SLI is a measurable indicator of service quality.

Common SLIs:
- request latency
- availability
- error rate
- throughput
- saturation

## SLO - Service Level Objective
An SLO is the target value or range for an SLI.

Example:
- SLI: latency
- SLO: 95% of requests should complete within 100 ms

## SLA - Service Level Agreement
An SLA is a formal agreement between a service provider and a customer.
It usually includes commitments based on SLOs.

## Quick summary
- SLI measures service performance.
- SLO defines the target level.
- SLA is the contractual commitment backed by the SLO.
